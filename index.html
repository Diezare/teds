7-step selection method

Before you download any plan, run through these quick checks. It reduces mistakes, wasted wood, and half-finished projects.

1

Is this for learning (practice), home use, or something that must look ‚Äúfurniture-grade‚Äù?

2

Plan around your real space: cutting area, assembly area, and where you‚Äôll clamp/glue.

3

Start with butt joints, pocket holes, or basic dados‚Äîsave advanced joinery for later builds.

4

Match the plan to your tools

If you don‚Äôt have a table saw, pick a plan that can be done with a circular saw + guide (or pre-cut lumber).

5

Estimate time realistically

Add buffer: cutting (1x), sanding (2x), finishing (2‚Äì3 coats). Beginners always underestimate finishing.

6

Check materials before you buy

Prefer common species and standard dimensions for your first builds. It‚Äôs cheaper and easier to replace mistakes.

7

Start with a ‚Äúconfidence build‚Äù

Pick a small project to validate your measuring/cutting workflow, then scale up.
